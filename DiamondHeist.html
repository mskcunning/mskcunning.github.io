📋 Incident Report — Case Summary

A high-profile diamond heist has rocked the city. A suspect has been apprehended and is currently in custody — but the stolen diamond is still missing, and we don't know if they acted alone.

A backpack was recovered at the scene. After a physical search, investigators discovered a USB flash drive disguised as a pen. This device may hold the key to the entire operation.

Your digital forensics team has been called in. You have the skills. You have the tools. It's time to find the truth.

Mission Objectives
01
🔍 Did They Do It?
Determine whether the suspect committed the crime. Find digital evidence that places them at the scene or links them to the heist.
02
🧩 How Was It Done?
Reconstruct how the heist was engineered. Trace the digital trail — encrypted files, emails, ciphers, and hidden data.
03
👥 Accomplices?
Determine if the suspect worked alone or had help. Look for communications, IP traces, and patterns that reveal a network of co-conspirators.
04
💎 Where Is the Diamond?
The stolen diamond is still missing. Use your forensic findings to uncover its location before it disappears for good.

Team Skills Inventory

Each team member should own one of the following skill areas. Document your process in your notes.

Skill / Technology Brief Description Process Overview
Cipher Cracking
 Decoding encrypted messages using substitution, transposition, or other cipher methods. Identify cipher type → Apply decryption method → Verify readable output
Encrypting Containers
 Securing files and drives using encryption tools; also decrypting evidence containers. Identify encrypted container → Obtain/crack key → Mount & extract contents
Pattern & Keyword Search
 Scanning files and drives for specific terms, metadata, or suspicious patterns. Define keywords → Run search tool → Catalog & analyze results
Chain of Custody
 Completing legal documentation tracking every item of evidence from discovery to court. Tag evidence → Fill custody form → Log every transfer with signature & time
Drive Imaging
 Creating a bit-for-bit copy of a drive or folder to preserve original evidence. Write-block original → Use imaging tool → Hash-verify the copy
Email Tracing
 Analyzing email headers to trace origin, route, and authenticity of messages. Extract full headers → Identify originating IP → Geolocate & verify
