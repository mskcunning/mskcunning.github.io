Steganalysis

Analyzing data for hidden content. Three categories: detection (knowing it exists), decoding (extracting it), and erasing (destroying it without the key).

ADS — Insertion Method

NTFS Alternate Data Streams attach hidden data to a file without changing its size. They don't transfer to non-NTFS systems — which limits and reveals them.

Watermarking vs. Stego

Digital watermarking targets Integrity in the CIA triad — it embeds a small, permanent ownership signature. Steganography targets Confidentiality. Same technique, opposite goals.

Essential Vocabulary
Steganography
Hiding data inside other data so its existence is concealed. From Greek: "hidden writing."
Covert
The secret message being hidden — not visible to a casual observer of the carrier.
Overt
The innocent-looking carrier (image, spam email, shaved head) containing the hidden message.
LSB Substitution
Replacing the least significant bit of each RGB channel with hidden data — a 1-bit shift across 16M+ colors is invisible to the eye.
ADS (NTFS)
Alternate Data Streams — hidden data attached to a file without changing its visible size. NTFS file system only.
Steganalysis
The science of detecting hidden content. Three categories: detection, decoding, erasing.
Generation Stego
The covert message drives generation of an innocent-looking overt message — like spam email text.
Digital Watermark
A small permanent embedded signature proving ownership. Targets Integrity in the CIA triad, not Confidentiality.
